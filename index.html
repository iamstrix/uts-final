The Path We Leave Behind

"I realized that in growth, we must not look back on our trail, but rather think of the people who will follow. Let them see how we traversed, let them see the struggles during our journey."

Growth is often viewed as a solitary mountain climb, where the only goal is to reach the peak. However, the true essence of 'Understanding the Self' lies in the realization that our journey serves as a map for those who come after us. It is not enough to simply bloom; we must acknowledge the soil, the storms, and the seasons that made that blooming possible.

This requirement is an honest documentation of my traverse. I have chosen not to hide the struggles, for it is in the struggle that the most profound self-knowledge is found. By sharing how I navigated the complexities of identity, culture, and belief, I hope to leave a trail that is not just visible, but meaningful—a testament that the path to the self is rarely linear, but always worth the climb.

IV

The Weight of Expectation

"Success is not final, failure is not fatal: it is the courage to continue that counts."

During the midterm, I established a SMART Goal to achieve high academic marks across all my subjects. I wanted to prove my capabilities and meet the standards I had set for myself. However, as I reflect on this journey now, I must admit that I was unable to fulfill that goal.

There was a period where the weight of others' expectations, combined with my own unrelenting pressure, became overwhelming. I found myself unable to bridge the gap between where I was and where I felt I needed to be. This led to a cycle of underperformance that lasted for several weeks—a time when the mental and emotional toll became visible in my work and my well-being.

What I learned about myself during this difficult traverse is that my worth is not solely defined by a letter grade. I learned that growth often requires us to falter so we can understand our limits and redefine what 'success' truly looks like. To make this happen next time, I need to prioritize self-compassion and realistic pacing over the pursuit of perfection. The struggle is not a sign of failure, but a necessary part of the path I am leaving behind.

XI

A Final Note on the Trail

As I close this chapter of 'Understanding the Self', I do not simply walk away from the person I was. I leave behind a trail—deliberately marked with the signs of my own growth and the scars of my struggles.

My greatest realization is that the self is never a finished product, but a continuous journey of becoming. To those who might one day find themselves where I once stood: do not be afraid of the steep paths or the moments when you lose your way. These are the parts of your map that will eventually guide someone else. My journey was not easy, but it was honest, and that honesty is what I offer as I move forward.

To my future self: I promise to never smooth over the rough edges of our history. I thank you for the courage to traverse the difficult terrain so that the version of us yet to come can walk with more certainty. This subject has taught me that while the trail behind us is fixed, the horizon ahead is wide, and I am ready to keep climbing.
